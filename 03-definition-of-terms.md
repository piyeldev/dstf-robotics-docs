

# 📖 Definition of Terms

**You can skip** this part and proceed to the next sections. But if you'd like, you can go through the terms to have a little background knowledge.

### Parallel Circuit
A circuit in which parts are connected through same terminals. In batteries, for example, positive of battery A is connected to the positive of battery B, and negative of battery A is connected to the negative of battery B. In batteries, this creates a greater capacity. The general rule is that, voltage stays the same, and currents either split or add.

### Series Circuit
A circuit in which parts are connected through the opposite terminals. In batteries, for example, positive of battery A is connected to negative of battery B (don't connect the other terminals together, it may explode ⚠️), and this will double the voltage. The general rule is that, the current stays the same, and voltage either divide or add.

### Gearbox
A gearbox (or transmission) is a mechanical unit containing a gear train that transfers power from a source (like an dc motor) to another component (like wheels), controlling torque and rotational speed.

### Torque
Torque is a measure of the rotational force (or "twisting" power) acting on an object. In the context of this project, torque refers to the amount of weight a motor can drive something without stalling. Higher torque, means higher twisting poster, means motor can drive greater weights without stalling.

### Stalling
Motor stalling means that the electric motor has more load on it than it is designed for and it can no longer supply enough torque (rotational power) to keep it spinning.

### nominal
"nominal" represent the ideal, expected, or target level of operation under normal conditions.

### GPIO
GPIOs are the physical metal pins sticking out of the board (or look like plastic slots, called female headers). They act as the primary bridge between your software code and the physical hardware world.

### Serial Communication
Serial Communication refers to Universal Asynchronous Receiver-Transmitter (UART).
It transfers data bit-by-bit sequentially over a two-wire interface consisting of a Transmit (TX) line and a Receive (RX) line.
Think of it as sending a long word letter-by-letter down a single wire, rather than trying to send all the letters at the same time down multiple wires.
This protocol is also used for debugging.

### MOSFET
A MOSFET is an electronic high-speed water valve, but instead of controlling water, it uses a tiny voltage to control a massive rush of electricity.
