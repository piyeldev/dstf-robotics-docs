](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1T_vZmyBlvhjeLJpllFRJ5p8uDwEyDlsU9Yc5tlmai6E/edit?usp=sharing)


<img src="/assets/robot.png" width="300px" height="330px">
This project was designed to solve the problem of stagnant water accumulation after heavy rainfalls around the SHS and JHS building grounds in AMNHS. The robot collects water using a pump and stores it in a simple container. Another pump is then used to irrigate the stored water through a hose. A servo motor helps distribute the water evenly during irrigation.

The robot can move forward, backward, left, and right using four geared motors with differential steering. It uses Wi-Fi communication to connect the robot to an app on the operator’s phone for wireless control.

The system is powered by two 18650 Li-ion batteries (7.4V, 2200mAh) and can operate for about one hour or less during continuous use, based on testing.
